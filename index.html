Research Profile

Building visual intelligence across structure, memory, and generation.

My current work studies how models can use complementary signals such as depth, temporal behavior, and controllable generation to improve robustness in real-world visual and learning scenarios.

I am especially interested in multimodal feature integration, efficient adaptation of foundation models, experimental evaluation, and production-aware AI systems.

Selected Research

Projects With a Clear Modeling Question

ECCV 2026 submission - under review

Depth-Assisted Skeleton Detection in Natural Images

Co-authored DASkel, a depth-assisted framework for skeleton detection that addresses RGB-only degradation under complex image content.

  • Dual-stream RGB-D architecture with separate DINOv3 backbones.
  • MoLA fine-tuning for efficient adaptation.
  • Multi-orientation context module for feature selection and fusion.
QRS workshop submission - under review

NLMEM-Transformer-MoE for Memory Retention Prediction

Built a hybrid statistical and deep-learning framework for personalized review recommendation from sparse learning-event sequences.

  • Nonlinear Mixed Effects Models for individual learning differences.
  • Coefficient-gated Mixture-of-Experts Transformer routing.
  • Temporal encodings for sparse timestamp behavior.
Generative modeling system

Stable Diffusion-Based Garment Editing and Generation

Fine-tuned diffusion models for clothing generation and editing under limited data, integrating LoRA and DragDiffusion into a web-facing interface.
