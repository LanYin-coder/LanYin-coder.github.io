About

I am an undergraduate student in Data Science and Big Data Technology at Wuhan Textile University. My research interests center on multimodal representation learning, audio-visual computing, RGB-D visual modeling, generative modeling, and visual computing.

My recent work studies how complementary visual signals and efficient adaptation methods can improve robustness in perception and generation systems. I am particularly interested in multimodal feature integration, experimental evaluation, model architecture design, and research systems that remain practical beyond controlled benchmarks.

Research

Research Projects

Depth-Assisted Skeleton Detection

Co-authored DASkel, a depth-assisted framework for skeleton detection that uses RGB-D modeling to reduce the degradation of RGB-only methods under complex visual content.

  • Dual-stream RGB-D architecture with separate DINOv3 backbones.
  • MoLA fine-tuning and multi-orientation context integration.
  • Experiments across mainstream skeleton detection datasets.

NLMEM-Transformer-MoE for Memory Retention Prediction

Built a hybrid statistical and deep-learning framework for personalized memory-state estimation and review recommendation from sparse learning event sequences.

  • Nonlinear Mixed Effects Models for individual learning differences.
  • Coefficient-gated Mixture-of-Experts Transformer routing.
  • Temporal encoding for sparse timestamp behavior.

Stable Diffusion-Based Garment Editing and Generation

Fine-tuned diffusion models for clothing generation and editing under limited-data conditions, integrating LoRA and DragDiffusion into a web-facing interface for interactive image manipulation.
